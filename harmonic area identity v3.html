The ledger

TheoremWith P at OD ∩ (B–2), the upper triangle OAD splits as OAB + OBP + BPD and the lower as OP2 + leftover; the shared OBP cancels, leaving OAB + BPD = OP2 — exactly, for every B.

Reciprocal ladderThe line from the corner to height n on the left edge meets the top at x = 2(n−1)/n, whose value is 1/n. So the fan to the corner cuts the top into 1, ½, ⅓…→0, and each fan-line crosses OD at OP2 = n/(n+1). The ladder and the area law are one construction.

√ ladderThe bottom carries the partner scale at x = √n, so the squared position reads the integer n — the additive register against the reciprocal one above. ThalesThe semicircle on AD supplies the geometric mean √(AB·BD) at B, the multiplicative bridge between them.
