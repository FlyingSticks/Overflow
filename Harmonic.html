Complete quadrangle · the diagonal point Q

The midpoint that outlives measure

Q is where the diagonals cross. In a parallelogram it sits at the metric ½ between the parallels. Open one pair of sides — then both — and Q slides off the metric midline, yet the cross-ratio (A, C ; Q, S) holds at −1 the whole way. The ½ that persists is harmonic, read off the crossing alone; the ½ that walks away was only ever a ruler.

Ledger

Fixed
Vertices A and D. The whole figure is built from joins and meets only — no length is ever measured.
Invariant
(A, C ; Q, S) = −1 at every position, guaranteed by the harmonic property of the complete quadrangle — a projective theorem, indifferent to metric.
Moves
R comes in from infinity (Hybrid), then P (Perspective). The line PR descends from the line at infinity to a finite horizon. S slides in along AC exactly as Q leaves the metric midpoint M.
Shows
“midpoint by crossing diagonals” ≠ “midpoint by measure,” except in the affine case — where they coincide only because S is the ideal point of AC, and the harmonic conjugate of infinity is the ordinary midpoint.
