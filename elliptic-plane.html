The Elliptic Plane ℝP²

The sphere with opposite points fused into one. Locally it is the sphere — same curvature, same little triangles. Everything that distinguishes it is global, and it all follows from a single fold.

The flat picture — the disk model

The elliptic plane is a disk whose opposite edge-points are the same point (matched letters). The gluing carries a flip, which is why the surface is one-sided.

A line runs edge to edge and closes into a loop — leave one side and you reappear at the point directly across (watch the dot). Two lines still cross at exactly one point.

And a line never cuts the disk in two: step across the glued edge and the halves are joined.

The bookkeeping of one fold Because the fold is two-to-one, the quantities halve: a full circle's 2π becomes a line's π; the total area 4π becomes 2π; the Euler characteristic 2 becomes 1. And delete a single line from the elliptic plane — what is left is the affine plane, the picture plane we began with, now completed.
