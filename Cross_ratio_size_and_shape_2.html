The cross-ratio: size and shape

Cut PQ twice and write a = PC, b = CQ, c = PD, d = DQ. The rectangle of width c + d and height a + b falls into four pieces — (a + b)(c + d) = ac + ad + bc + bd. The harmonic condition wears two costumes at once, and they turn out to be a single event.

1 · One event, two costumes — drag C and D

The two complements ad, bc (off the diagonal) and the two diagonal-rectangles ac, bd (the green diagonal runs through them). When the inner point X lands on the diagonal, the complements match in size and the diagonal-rectangles match in shape — both at the same instant, witnessed by the two corner-diagonals lining up straight. Here same shape means the same proportions in the same orientation — equivalently, the corner-diagonals parallel; a rotated copy (a 2:1 against a 1:2) does not count.

2 · The bookkeeping — quotient vs product

Two ratios live in this picture, and only one is a cross-ratio. The harmonic example below (a = +0.25, b = +0.75, c = −0.50, d = +1.50) has P exactly on the diagonal.

cross-ratio   λ = (a/b) ÷ (c/d) = ad / bc  — the quotient of the size pair. Harmonic ⇒ λ = −1.
shape ratio   ac / bd = (a/b) × (c/d)  — the product of the same two cut-ratios.
conservation   ad · bc = ac · bd = abcd  — always, for any position of the cuts.

The quotient and the product are different animals. ad / bc is a genuine cross-ratio — invariant under every projective (Möbius) map of the four points, which is exactly why the harmonic value −1 is a real, drawable number. ac / bd is only an affine shape ratio — it survives shifting and scaling but is changed by inversion, so it is not a cross-ratio. The conservation law says the four sub-areas are never independent: the product of the two complements always equals the product of the two diagonal-rectangles. What harmonic adds on top is the one extra condition that the complements also match — and matched size, matching proportions, and straight corner-diagonals are three names for it.

Convention: λ = (C−P)(D−Q) ⁄ (C−Q)(D−P), with P, Q the frame. On the diagonal ⇒ |λ| = 1 (harmonic −1, or coincident +1); the sign is the inside/outside colour, not the diagonal. All values verified numerically before build. flyingsticks · Anharmonic Interface.
