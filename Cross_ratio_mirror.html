The cross-ratio, on a line — and the mirror in it

Above: the four signed steps, paired crosswise, working out to −1. Below: the same picture read as a mirror — Q's cut swung around to S, the same 1 : 2 from the inside and from the outside.

The lower line is the same four points, read as a mirror. Q's cut and S's cut are the same shape — 1 : 2 — but Q takes it from inside the span and S from outside, so one reads +½ and the other −½. Equal size, opposite sign; their ratio is the −1 you found up top. Swing Q's cut around to the far side and it lands on S — that swing is the harmonic mirror.
