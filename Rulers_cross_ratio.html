Coordinates on a line · the cross-ratio

Lay any ruler you like — it doesn't notice

Four points sit at fixed places on one line. Read them with a plain tape, then a shifted and rescaled one, then a warped one. The four readings change completely — but the cross-ratio (A, C ; Q, S) comes out −1 every time.

Same four points throughout — only the tape changes. The vertical guides carry each point down to its reading on every ruler, and the line beneath each tape works the value out. The cross-ratio is what survives every re-reading, including the projective one a perspective view performs.
