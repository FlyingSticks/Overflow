The cross-ratio as ad = ±bc

Two points C and D each cut the same segment PQ. Write a = PC, b = CQ, c = PD, d = DQ. Then the cross-ratio is λ = (a/b)/(c/d) = ad/bc. The whole story lives in two values of λ.

1 · Two cuts, one segment — drag C and D

+1: the two cut-ratios are equal (ad = bc) → C and D are the same point; the figure collapses.  −1: equal in size, opposite in sign (ad = −bc) → one cut inside, one outside — the harmonic pair.

2 · The same balance, drawn as area

Euclid's equal complements (Elements I.43): the very same a, b, c, d as two shaded corners of a rectangle cut by its diagonal. The box is drawn to scale — its width is |c| + |d|, its height is |a| + |b|, so the two shaded areas are literally |ad| and |bc| at one common scale. Equal areas ⇔ the inner point sits on the diagonal ⇔ |λ| = 1, and the colour carries the sign. Drag C and D and watch the box reshape; with C inside, its height stays fixed and the cuts trade width while the areas balance.

3 · The harmonic involution — λ locked at −1

Now D is forced to be the harmonic conjugate of C, so λ = −1 always. Drag C inside the segment and watch D answer from outside. The minus sign is that inside↔outside flip.
