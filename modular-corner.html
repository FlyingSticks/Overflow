The modular corner

The whole story has one root at the top. The modular group is ℤ/2 ∗ ℤ/3 — it carries exactly two kinds of rotation, of order 2 and order 3, and no others. Their fixed points are the only special points of the picture: i and ρ. And the fixed-point equation of the order-3 one is τ² − τ + 1 = 0 — the equianharmonic polynomial itself.

from the top · the torsion of the modular group

The answer is 2 and 3, because the group has nothing else

PSL(2, ℤ) = ⟨ S, T | S² = (ST)³ = 1 ⟩ ≅ ℤ/2 ∗ ℤ/3 → torsion elements of order 2 and 3 only → two elliptic points: i (order 2), ρ = e^{iπ/3} (order 3) → j sends them, with the cusp, to 1728, 0, ∞ → order-3 fixed-point equation: τ² − τ + 1 = 0 ≡ equianharmonic cross-ratio: λ² − λ + 1 = 0

This is not an analogy between two things that resemble each other. The λ that appears here is the cross-ratio — the modular lambda function is the same λ, a coordinate on the four marked points. Its six branches over j are the six cosets of Γ(2) in the full group, and those cosets are S₃, the very symmetry that permutes the cross-ratio's six values. So the equianharmonic value sits at the order-3 corner not by coincidence but by identity: λ(ρ) = e±iπ/3.

And that closes the suite. The order-3 point ρ is where the regular tetrahedron lives on the sphere, where the vesica's two circles cross, where the six cross-ratio values collapse to two. Every one of those was a shadow of a single group-theoretic fact: PSL(2, ℤ) has an order-3 generator whose fixed-point equation is x² − x + 1. Ask "why eiπ/3 and nothing else," and this is the floor the question rests on.

Proven at the root

Theorem — the domain

The quotient ℍ / PSL(2, ℤ) has exactly two elliptic points — i of order 2 and ρ of order 3 — and one cusp. Under the modular function j they map to 1728, 0, ∞. Verified here: j computed from Jacobi theta series agrees with the q-expansion, and gives j(i) = 1728, j(ρ) = 0 to machine precision.

Theorem — the bridge to the cross-ratio

The order-3 generator TS fixes ρ, and its fixed-point equation is τ² − τ + 1 = 0, identical to the equianharmonic condition λ² − λ + 1 = 0 whose roots are e±iπ/3. Since λ(τ) is the cross-ratio and Γ(2)\PSL(2,ℤ) ≅ S₃, the value at the order-3 corner is λ(ρ) = e±iπ/3: the same number as the tetrahedron and the vesica apex.

Note — what this explains

This answers the "why these and no others" that the earlier panels raised but could not settle from inside themselves. The special values are the images under j of the torsion of the modular group — two orders, 2 and 3, and nothing else. The tetrahedron, the vesica, and the six-value collapse are consequences; this is the cause.
