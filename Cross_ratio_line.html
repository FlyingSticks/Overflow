The cross-ratio, on a line

Four signed steps along one line. Two of them multiply on top, two on the bottom — paired crosswise. The whole thing compares how Q and S split the span between the anchors A and C.

Every piece is a signed step — length and direction. Plum is the numerator's pair, teal the denominator's, and the pairing runs crosswise (the little X). The one step that points left, A − Q, is what makes the answer negative — and that minus is exactly the picture of Q inside the span and S outside it.
