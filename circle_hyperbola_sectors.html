The circle (+) and the hyperbola (−)

One slider sets a single number. On the unit circle x² + y² = 1 it is the angle θ, and the sector has area θ/2. On the unit hyperbola x² − y² = 1 the same number is the rapidity φ, and that sector has area φ/2. Same construction, one sign flipped. The light cone v = ±1 is where the rapidity runs off to infinity; φ is half the log of the cross-ratio of the worldline with the two null rays, read on the shared vertex tangent. This is the companion to the area identity — the literal + versus −.

The ledger

TheoremThe unit circle and unit hyperbola are one equation x² + εy² = 1 with ε = ±1. The sector cut from the vertex to the parameter has area θ/2 when ε = +1 and φ/2 when ε = −1 — the semicircle measures the angle, the hyperbola measures the rapidity. The two are the same picture with the sign of the second square flipped, sharing centre, vertex, and the vertical tangent there.

Neutral surfaceThe asymptotes v = ±1 are the light cone: the boundary that belongs to neither the timelike nor the spacelike side. For the circle the matching null directions are the circular points, slopes ±i — imaginary. A real null pair gives a real measure (the boost, multiplier eφ); an imaginary null pair gives an imaginary measure (the rotation, multiplier eiθ). The signature sign ε is exactly this swap.

DiscriminantA boost is the hyperbolic Möbius map of the line of directions: two real fixed points (the nulls), real multiplier D = e2φ. A rotation is the elliptic Möbius map: fixed points the circular points ±i, multiplier e2iθ. Same trichotomy as the conic classification — hyperbolic against elliptic, with the parabolic case (v = 1, a null direction) degenerate between them.

AnharmonicRapidity is the logarithm of an anharmonic ratio: φ = ½ ln D, where D is the cross-ratio of (rest, worldline; the two nulls), read where they pierce the vertex tangent. The Reciprocal involution λ → 1/λ sends D → 1/D, i.e. φ → −φ — boost reversal. The Complement λ → 1−λ moves the absolute, re-choosing which directions count as null — the step between the three Cayley–Klein geometries. So the six anharmonic values are these three standpoints (in two orientations), not six Lorentz operations.
