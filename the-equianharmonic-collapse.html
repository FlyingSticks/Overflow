The equianharmonic collapse:
six into two, where the ruler can't look

A cross-ratio has six values under reordering. The harmonic case merges them to three — and you can watch that happen on the real line. The equianharmonic case merges them to two, but only off the line, at ±60° on the unit circle. Drag λ and make the collapse happen.

Snap to equianharmonic: the six points merge in triples. The three rotations eσσ² stack at +60°, the three involutions pqr stack at −60°. Orbit 2, stabiliser 3 — and the stabiliser is exactly the rotation σ that spun the cast in The Ceiling.

why two · orbit–stabiliser, made spatial

The orbit shrinks because the stabiliser grows

The product is pinned at six. So fewer images means more symmetry holding λ still — never the reverse. The four configurations are the four ways six factors:

generic  6 × 1 — nothing but e fixes λ.
harmonic  3 × 2 — one involution fixes λ (which one depends on the point: −1↦q, ½↦p, 2↦r).
degenerate  3 × 2 — same count, different orbit: the points are {0, 1, ∞}, not {−1, ½, 2}.
equianharmonic  2 × 3 — the whole rotation subgroup ⟨σ⟩ fixes λ. The deepest collapse, and the only one that is not on the real line.

off the axis · what the ruler can't see

Harmonic lives on ℝ; equianharmonic needs √−3

Set λ real and all six images stay real — six marks strung along your original ruler. Push toward −1 and three pairs touch: the harmonic collapse, fully visible on the line. But orbit-size-2 demands a stabiliser of order 3, and the order-3 rotation's fixed points solve λ²−λ+1 = 0 — discriminant −3, no real roots. The two survivors are complex conjugates the ruler was never able to reach.

Kept — theorem, demonstrated

For every λ the action satisfies |orbit| · |stabiliser| = 6. Equianharmonic ⇔ stabiliser = ⟨σ⟩ (order 3) ⇔ orbit 2 ⇔ λ²−λ+1 = 0 ⇔ j = 0. Built here by counting coincidences against the maps, not asserted.

Struck — false rhyme

Orbit size 3 means harmonic. Two different orbits have size 3: the harmonic triple {−1, ½, 2} and the degenerate triple {0, 1, ∞}. Same count is not the same configuration — the j-value separates them (1728 vs ∞).

The three special j-values

The collapses are exactly the three points where the j-line is special: j = 0 (equianharmonic, orbit 2), j = 1728 (harmonic, orbit 3), j = ∞ (degenerate, orbit 3). Everywhere else the orbit is a full six and j is unremarkable.
