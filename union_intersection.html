Two lines · the meet

Union and intersection — either, and both

The same pair of crossed lines, AC and BD, read two ways. Union lights every point on either line — the whole X. Intersection keeps only the points on both — the single crossing, Q.

The inversion is the thing to keep: the permissive rule (either) yields the larger object, both whole lines; the strict rule (both) yields the smaller, a single point. Reading the crossing as AC ∩ BD is exactly the projective meet — and being a plain static drawing, this one renders with JavaScript on or off.
