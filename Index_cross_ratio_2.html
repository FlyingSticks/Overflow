The cross-ratio — from the segment to the sphere

Two cuts on a segment, the single number they make — and the sphere where its six values live.

Cut a segment twice and the cross-ratio λ = (a/b)/(c/d) = ad/bc tells the whole story; the harmonic value λ = −1 is where the figure balances. Reorder the four points and that one number becomes six, whose natural home is the Riemann sphere — the real, drawable values ring the equator, and the value goes complex only at the poles. Four pieces, in reading order: the harmonic balance, its size-and-shape reading, a short written note, and the sphere where the value lives.
